agní MW 1899
agní m. (√ ag, Uṇ.)
  1. fire, sacrificial fire (of three kinds, Gārhapatya, Āhavanīya, and Dakṣiṇa)
  2. the number three, Sūryas.
  3. the god of fire, the fire of the stomach, digestive faculty, gastric fluid
  4. bile, L.
  5. gold, L.
  6. N. of various plants — Semecarpus Anacardium, Suśr.; Plumbago Zeylanica and Rosea; Citrus Acida
  7. a mystical substitute for the letter r
  8. in the Kātantra grammar, N. of noun-stems ending in i and u
agniḥ AP90 1890
agniḥ [Uṇ. 4.50]
  1. Fire
  2. the God of fire
  3. sacrificial fire of three kinds (Gārhapatya, Āhavanīya and Dakṣiṇa)
  4. the fire of the stomach, digestive faculty, gastric fluid
  5. bile
  6. cauterization
  7. gold
  8. the number three
  9. N. of various plants (Plumbago Zeylanica; Semecarpus Anacardium; Citrus Acida)
  10. a mystical substitute for the letter r

Proposal C: Minimal Lookup

The cleanest student-facing version: one box, sensible defaults, optional scholarly controls.

Lowest learning curve

Advanced Panel
